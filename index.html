About Me

I'm an environmental scientist dedicated to understanding and mitigating the impacts of climate change. My research focuses on nature-based solutions and the complex interactions between forests, fire, and the carbon cycle.

From studying post-fire forest recovery to modeling carbon sequestration potential, I work at the intersection of ecology, climate science, and policy. I believe that rigorous science combined with effective communication is essential for driving meaningful environmental action.

I received my PhD at the University of Arizona in Geosciences. I studied paleoclimate and paleoecology focusing on the past 10,000-20,000 years of climate and vegetation change.

When I'm not working I love ultrarunning, trail running, disc golf, reading, and music.
